Endo-Power is dosed at one of two levels — low or high (maximum) — based on the diet’s Hemicellulose NSP: the enzyme-degradable cell-wall hemicellulose. The diet is moved to the high (maximum) dose when Hemicellulose NSP reaches the species threshold. This is an internal guideline, grounded in the literature on hemicellulose composition and NSP-enzyme response [H1–H6] and on NSP / fibre level and performance [P1–P7, S1–S7]; it is not a single published cut-off.

High (maximum) dose  if  Hemicellulose NSP ≥ species threshold
Hemicellulose NSP = arabinoxylan (xylose + arabinose) + β-glucan + mannan / galactomannan (mannose + galactose). These are the principal anti-nutritive, cell-wall hemicelluloses of feedstuffs and the direct substrates of Endo-Power’s xylanase, β-glucanase and β-mannanase. The literature consistently identifies arabinoxylan and β-glucan (with mannans) as the main NSP that depress digestibility and respond to NSP-enzymes [H2,H3,H4,H5,H6]. Cellulose and pectic uronic acids are excluded, because Endo-Power has no cellulase or pectinase; counting them over-states the expected response for high-cellulose / high-pectin materials such as sugar beet pulp [H6]. Oligosaccharides (raffinose-family) are also excluded from this fraction; the α-galactosidase activity still addresses them, but they are a minor, chemically-distinct fraction and do not drive the threshold.

Why hemicellulose NSP, and why species-specific. The main NSP of cereal grains and their by-products are β-glucan, arabinoxylan and insoluble cellulose [H2]; of these, arabinoxylan and β-glucan (plus mannans in meals such as palm kernel and soybean) are the hemicelluloses that raise digesta viscosity, encapsulate nutrients and depress performance, and that NSP-enzymes actually hydrolyse [H3,H4,H5]. Cellulose, by contrast, is largely not degraded by xylanase/glucanase/mannanase and passes through, so it should not drive enzyme dosing [H6]. Basing the dose on the hemicellulose fraction matches dose to expected response. Thresholds are set lower for poultry (short, fast, viscosity-sensitive gut) than for pigs, which tolerate more fibre through hind-gut fermentation [S4,S6].

These are internal guideline thresholds, not single published cut-off values. On this tool’s basis they give a clear differentiation: corn–soybean meal diets fall below threshold (low / standard dose), while wheat-, barley-, rye- and by-product-based diets exceed it (high dose). For example a corn-SBM broiler diet has a hemicellulose NSP of about 5.8% (low), a wheat or wheat-barley broiler diet about 8.3–8.8% (high), and high-fibre breeding diets clearly higher. For gestating sows and boars a high reading is a high-dose candidate rather than an automatic high dose, since fibre is often included on purpose for satiety. If you would rather default most commercial diets to the high dose, lower these thresholds; if you want only viscous-cereal and by-product diets to trigger high, keep or raise them.

Disclaimer. The dose level is set on a Hemicellulose-NSP basis — the enzyme-degradable cell-wall hemicellulose (arabinoxylan + β-glucan + mannan/galactomannan). The species hemicellulose-NSP thresholds (7.0–8.5% of diet) are internal guideline thresholds, grounded in the hemicellulose-composition and NSP-enzyme literature [H1–H6] and directionally supported by the NSP / performance literature, but they are not single published cut-off values. Poultry thresholds are set below pig thresholds because pigs tolerate more NSP via hind-gut fermentation. Ingredient NSP values are corrected to literature totals (Bach Knudsen 1997; Knudsen 2014; Choct 1997). Total NSP (shown alongside as a fibre-burden indicator) additionally includes oligosaccharides and a cellulose / other term (cellulose plus pectic uronic acids); cellulose / pectin are not Endo-Power substrates and are therefore excluded from Hemicellulose NSP and from the dose decision. For gestating sows and boars a high reading is a high-dose candidate, since fibre is often included intentionally. Final inclusion should account for age, diet energy specification, ingredient dry-matter, and the matrix value applied in formulation. This tool does not replace product datasheets or technical-service recommendations.
Method. Diet NSP component = Σ (ingredient inclusion % / 100 × ingredient NSP % DM). This treats inclusions and NSP values on the same (dry) basis, which is accurate for the dry ingredients used here; for a strict as-fed value each term should be multiplied by the ingredient DM fraction, so diets containing wet by-products may need DM correction. Ingredient NSP values are set to literature totals (Bach Knudsen 1997; Knudsen 2014, Poult Sci 93:2380; Choct 1997) and include a cellulose / other term covering cellulose, pectic uronic acids and other non-hemicellulosic NSP; this term is part of total NSP but is not hydrolysed by Endo-Power (no cellulase / pectinase). Enzyme–substrate mapping: Xylanase → xylose & arabinose (arabinoxylan); β-Glucanase → β-glucan; β-Mannanase → mannose & galactose (galactomannan); α-Galactosidase → stachyose, verbascose, raffinose. The remainder below 100% inclusion is treated as non-NSP ingredients (fat, minerals, premix).
